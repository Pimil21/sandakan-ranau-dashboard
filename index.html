üìñ About This Project

This research explores the emotional landscape of the 1945 Sandakan-Ranau Death Marches through innovative integration of Natural Language Processing (NLP) and Geographic Information Systems (GIS). By extracting emotion-laden expressions from historical narratives and mapping them to geographical coordinates, this study reveals the spatial-temporal-emotional patterns of one of World War II's most tragic events in 1945 Borneo, Malaysian Pacific theater.

Thesis Title: "Emotion Mapping and Sentiment Analysis using NLP and GIS: The Case Study of the Sandakan-Ranau Death Marches (1945)"
